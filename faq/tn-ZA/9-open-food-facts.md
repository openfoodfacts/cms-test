## What is the difference with other web sites, services and mobile applications that already allow to view food products information?

The main difference is for us a critical one: our data is freely available to all and for all uses. It's what is called open data.

Almost all other sites, services and applications forbid others from reproducting and reusing their data. Quite to the contrary, they jealously keep it for themselves. Mo e ka nnang mo maemong otlhe, melawana ya bone ya tirelo e thibela ka tlhamalalo tiriso epe e e seng ya botho le go ntsha tshedimosetso yotlhe kgotsa dikarolo tsa tshedimosetso.

We consider that food products information is too important and useful to keep it locked in a safe. Ka jalo fa re ne re simolola porojeke ya Open Food Facts le app ya selulafouno, morago ka 2012, re ne ra swetsa ka gore re dire se se fapaaneng le seo: ga re letle fela tiriso le tiriso gape ya polokelotshedimosetso ya rona, ka kgololesego le kwantle ga tuediso, mo go mongwe le mongwe le mo tirisong yotlhe (go akaretsa le ya kgwebo), mme gape re e rotloetsa!

Go dira gore tshedimosetso e nne teng phatlalatsa (se se bidiwang _tshedimosetso e e bulegileng_) go letla batho ka bongwe, mekgatlho, dikhamphani, babatlisisi jj go tswa kwa lefatsheng lotlhe go akanya le go tlhama ditiriso tsa tshedimosetso tse ruri re neng re ka se akanye ka tsone.

## What is Open Food Facts' economic model

Contributors are volunteers. Their contributions are gathered in an open database that can be used by everyone and for all uses. (bona [Melawana ya tiriso gape](https://en.openfoodfacts.org/terms-of-use#reuse))

Mongwe le mongwe (go akaretsa mme e seng fela ba ba nang le seabe le batlhami ba Open Food Facts) ka jalo a ka anamisa gape le/kgotsa a dirisa tshedimosetso gape go aga diwebosaete, ditirelo, dirweboleta, ditiriso tsa selulafouno, kgotsa go kwala diathikele le dithutopatlisiso. They are free to make the resulting work freely available, or to sell or monetize it (e.g. with ads), as long as they respect the terms of reuse.

We are also strictly independent from the food industry, and all the services and software we build are free. Ka sekai, Polatefomo ya rona ya Batlhagisi ga e duelelwe gotlhelele, mme re itumelela seo le go feta ka gonne re akanya gore se ka nna sa ba thusa go tokafatsa dikumo tsa bone.

## Can I add product pictures or data from the manufacturer's site, shopping sites or other sites?

Probably not. Almost all other sites forbid reproduction and reuse of their data and images, and that is actually the reason why we have created Open Food Facts: to make all this data available to all and for all uses.

To avoid any legal problem, we therefore ask contributors to only add pictures that they took themselves, and only data that is coming from the product packaging and label.

## Is the information and data on products verified?

Tshedimosetso le tshedimosetso di romelwa ke batsayakarolo ba Dintlha tsa Dijo tse di Buletsweng. The contributors also send pictures of the product, its labels, ingredients lists and nutrition facts table. When in doubt, visitors can thus check the accuracy by themselves, and if there is an error, they can correct it on the spot.

## I am a food product manufacturer, can I add my own products?

Yes! The only condition is to accept that the data and the pictures be made available under an open licence. (bona [Dipeelano tsa moneelo](https://en.openfoodfacts.org/terms-of-use#contribution))

Re tlhamile polatefomo ya mahala go go letla go reka dinepe le tshedimosetso ka ga dikumo tsa gago bonolo, go sa kgathalesege gore o bogolo bo kana kang. It will let you import complete and up-to-date data and visuals into Open Food Facts that will be visible in more than 100 other nutritional apps. Additionaly, the platform provides suggestions to improve the Nutri-Score of your products. Ikgolaganye le rona go buisana ka gore re ka tswelela jang: [batlhagisi@openfoodfacts.org](mailto:producers@openfoodfacts.org)
