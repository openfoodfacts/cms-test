## What is the difference with other web sites, services and mobile applications that already allow to view food products information?

The main difference is for us a critical one: our data is freely available to all and for all uses. It's what is called open data.

Almost all other sites, services and applications forbid others from reproducting and reusing their data. Quite to the contrary, they jealously keep it for themselves. Maemong a mangata, melao ea bona ea ts'ebeletso e thibela ka ho hlaka tšebeliso efe kapa efe eo e seng ea botho le ho ntšoa ha data eohle kapa likarolo tsa data.

We consider that food products information is too important and useful to keep it locked in a safe. Kahoo ha re qala projeke ea Open Food Facts le sesebelisoa sa mohala, morao koana ka 2012, re ile ra etsa qeto ea ho etsa se fapaneng hantle: ha re lumelle feela tšebeliso le tšebeliso ea database ea rona, ka bolokolohi le ntle ho tefo, ho bohle le bakeng sa lits'ebeliso tsohle (ho kenyeletsoa le tsa khoebo), empa re boetse re e khothaletsa!

Ho etsa hore data e fumanehe phatlalatsa (seo se tsejoang e le _data e bulehileng_) ho lumella batho ka bomong, mekhatlo, lik'hamphani, bafuputsi jj. ho tsoa lefats'eng lohle ho nahana le ho nts'etsapele lits'ebetso tsa data eo re neng re ke ke ra nahana ka eona.

## What is Open Food Facts' economic model

Contributors are volunteers. Their contributions are gathered in an open database that can be used by everyone and for all uses. (sheba [Melao ea ho sebelisa hape](https://en.openfoodfacts.org/terms-of-use#reuse))

Motho e mong le e mong (ho kenyeletsoa le bafani le baqapi ba Open Food Facts empa ho sa felle feela ho bona) a ka aba le/kapa a sebedisa data hape ho aha diwebsaete, ditshebeletso, software, dikopo tsa mehala ya thekeng, kapa ho ngola dihlooho le dithuto. They are free to make the resulting work freely available, or to sell or monetize it (e.g. with ads), as long as they respect the terms of reuse.

We are also strictly independent from the food industry, and all the services and software we build are free. Mohlala, Sethala sa rona sa Bahlahisi ke mahala ka ho felletseng, 'me re thabetse seo le ho feta hobane re nahana hore se ka ba thusa ho ntlafatsa lihlahisoa tsa bona.

## Can I add product pictures or data from the manufacturer's site, shopping sites or other sites?

Probably not. Almost all other sites forbid reproduction and reuse of their data and images, and that is actually the reason why we have created Open Food Facts: to make all this data available to all and for all uses.

To avoid any legal problem, we therefore ask contributors to only add pictures that they took themselves, and only data that is coming from the product packaging and label.

## Is the information and data on products verified?

Tlhahisoleseding le tlhahisoleseding di romelwa ke bafani ba Open Food Facts. The contributors also send pictures of the product, its labels, ingredients lists and nutrition facts table. When in doubt, visitors can thus check the accuracy by themselves, and if there is an error, they can correct it on the spot.

To detect potential errors more easily, we will progressively add automated checks. mohlala, haeba lintlha tsa phepo ea sehlahisoa li fapane haholo le lihlahisoa tsa sehlopha se le seng, e ka ba phoso. If the nutriments add up to more than 100%, it's definitely an error.

Finally, many producers send us photos and data about their products using our Platform for Producers. Haeba u mohlahisi, ka kopo [ikopanye le rona](mailto:producers@openfoodfacts.org)

## I am a food product manufacturer, can I add my own products?

Yes! The only condition is to accept that the data and the pictures be made available under an open licence. (sheba [Melao ea monehelo](https://en.openfoodfacts.org/terms-of-use#contribution))

Re thehile sethala sa mahala ho o dumella ho kenya difoto le tlhahisoleseding e mabapi le dihlahiswa tsa hao habonolo, ho sa tsotelehe boholo ba hao. It will let you import complete and up-to-date data and visuals into Open Food Facts that will be visible in more than 100 other nutritional apps. Additionaly, the platform provides suggestions to improve the Nutri-Score of your products. Ikopanye le rona ho buisana ka hore na re ka tsoela pele joang: [producers@openfoodfacts.org](mailto:producers@openfoodfacts.org)
